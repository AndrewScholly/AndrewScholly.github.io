About

Early life

My life was mostly normal early on, growing up a fairly normal family, both of which had prepared quite well for me and my brother. We grew up as one big family, with some cute fluffs as well.

We went oversees for as few years due to work, and when we returned I started school at Conestoga Valley, at the time requiring a helper due to my Aspergers, but by middle school I no longer needed one.

Teenage years

After starting middle school, I was surprised at the amount of classes and yet how similar it felt to elementary school, one possible reason was how I often did things alone then, and instead of being split up like groups of friends might be, I just went to the next class, learn something for 45 minutes, go to next class, wash, rinse, repeat.

After several fairly normal, and frankly, a bit boring years of middle school, I headed on to high school, where I, like middle school, felt it was quite similar, and even easier in some places, such as less classes and the ability to bring backpacks wherever, causing the lockers to be rarely used.

Acomplishments

One of my more general achievments is the honour role during high school, which I have achievment many times in my many years at CV.

Another of my, perhaps the most impressive, was becoming an Eagle Scout in 2018, which took several years of work and planning, as well as many merit badges, including lifesaving and first aid, as well as swimming and space exoloration.

Future plans

While I may be fairly young, I still hope to learn and find a career path that would make me happy and make a good amount of money at the same time. I don't really wish for any kind of real fancy place to live or a big yacht or something, all I want is a fun job, friends/family who like me and spend time with me, and a place I can comfortably live in and spend a decent amount of time in.

For future careers, aside from the current college courses at Stevens, I am thinking of possible IT careers, as I've found working with people is incredibly more interesting and fun than without.
